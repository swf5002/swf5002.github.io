    • a. P(2) = 2! < 22
    • b. 2! = 2⋅1 = 2; 22 = 4; hence 2! < 22
    • c. k! < kk
    • d. (k+1)! < (k+1)k+1
    • e. Assume k! < kk

      (k + 1)k! < kk(k + 1)
      (k + 1)! < (k + 1)k(k + 1) = (k + 1)k+1

    • f. We have shown P(2) and we have shown that P(k+1) on the assumption of P(k). This is a proof by mathematical induction that P(n) holds for all n > 1.
  • 34. 6 | (n3 - n) for n ≥ 0

    BASE: 13 - 1 = 0 and 6 | 0
    INDUCTIVE:
    Assume: 6 | (k3 - k)
    Show: 6 | [(k + 1)3 - (k + 1)]
    (k + 1)3 - (k + 1) = k3 + 3k2 + 3k + 1 - k - 1
    = (k3 - k) + 3k2 + 3k
    By inductive assumption 6 | (k3 - k); it remains to show:
    6 | (3k2 + 3k) or 6 | 3k(k + 1); proof by cases:
    Case 1: k is even; then 3k is even and 6 | 3k(k + 1)
    Case 2: k is odd; then (k + 1) is even, and 6 | 3k(k + 1)
