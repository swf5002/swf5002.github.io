    Uniqueness proof:
    ASSUME: a, b, and c are real and a ≠ 0.
    SUPPOSE: there are two distinct solutions, d and e, for x in the equation ax + b = c.
    SHOW: d = e.
    ad + b = c and ae + b = c, by the supposition, then
    ad + b = ae + b, as they both equal c, and
    ad = ae, subtracting b from both sides, and
    d = e, dividing both sides by a.

  • 30.

    SHOW: there are no integers x, y, such that 2x2 + 5y2 = 14.
    Proof by cases; in each case we show the imposibility of BOTH x and y being integers.
    Focusing on y, we see that |y| must be < 2, as 5(22) = 5((-2)2) and both are 20 which is > 14. Hence y can only be -1, 0, or 1.

    Case 1: y is 1, then
    2x2 + 5 = 14, or
    2x2 = 9, and
    x2 = 9/2
    which is impossible because the square of no integer is 9/2.

    Case 2: y = 0, then
    2x2 = 14, and
    x2 = 7, and
    this is also impossible as the square of no integer is 7.

    Case 3: y = -1; this case is similar to Case 1.
