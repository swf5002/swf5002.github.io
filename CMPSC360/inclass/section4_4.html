  • 6.
    • a. Find the inverse of 2 mod 17. Note gcd(2,17) = 1

      17 = 8⋅2 + 1
      1 = 1⋅17 - 8⋅2
      Therefore -8 is the inverse of 2 mod 17.

    • b. Find the inverse of 34 mod 89. Euclidean determination of gcd:
      89 = 2⋅34 + 21
      34 = 1⋅21 + 13
      21 = 1⋅13 + 8
      13 = 1⋅8 + 5
      8 = 1⋅5 + 3
      5 = 1⋅3 + 2
      3 = 1⋅2 + 1
      2 = 2⋅1 + 0
      Therefore gcd(34,89) = 1.
      Working backwards:
      1 = 3 - 1⋅2
      2 = 5 - 1⋅3 ⇒ 1 = 3 - 1(5 - 1⋅3) = 2⋅3 - 1⋅5
      3 = 8 - 1⋅5 ⇒ 1 = 2(8 - 1⋅5) - 1⋅5 = 2⋅8 - 3⋅5
      5 = 13 - 1⋅8 ⇒ 1 = 2⋅8 - 3(13 - 1⋅8) = 5⋅8 - 3⋅13
      8 = 21 - 1⋅13 ⇒ 1 = 5(21 - 1⋅3) - 3⋅13 = 5⋅21 - 8⋅13
      13 = 34 - 1⋅21 ⇒ 1 = 5⋅21 - 8(34 - 1⋅21) = 13⋅21 - 8⋅34
      21 = 89 - 2⋅34 ⇒ 1 = 13(89 - 2⋅34) - 8⋅34 = 13⋅89 - 34⋅34
      Therefore, gcd(34,89) = 1 = 13⋅89 - 34⋅34 is the Bezout indentity.
      Check that 1 = 1157 - 1156.
      Therefore -34 is the inverse of 34 mod 89.

  • 10. 2x ≡ 7 (mod 17).

    -8⋅2x ≡ -8⋅7 (mod 17)
    x = -56 (mod 17)
    -56 = -4⋅ -56 = -68 + 12
    Therefore 12 (mod 7) is a solution, and so are:
    ... -9, -2, 5, 12, 19, 26, 33, ...

  • 20. Simultaneous solution of: x ≡ 2 (mod 3), x ≡ 1 (mod 4), and x ≡ 3 (mod 5)

    a1 = 2, a2 = 1, a3 = 3
    m = 3⋅4⋅5 = 60
    M1 = 60/3 = 20, M2 = 60/4 = 15, M3 = 60/5 = 12
    y1 = 2; 2 is an inverse of 20 (mod 3) because 2⋅20 = 3⋅13 + 1 ≡ 1 (mod 3)
    y2 = 3; 3 is an inverse of 15 (mod 4) because 3⋅15 = 4⋅11 + 1 ≡ 1 (mod 4)
    y3 = 3; 3 is an inverse of 12 (mod 5) because 3⋅12 = 5⋅7 + 1 ≡ 1 (mod 5)
    Then: 2⋅20⋅2 + 1⋅15⋅3 + 3⋅12⋅3 = 233, and
    233 = 3⋅60 + 53 ≡ 53 (mod 60)
    Therefore x = 53.
