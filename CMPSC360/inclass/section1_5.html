    F(x,y) = x can fool y; D = {all people}.

    • a. ∀xF(x,Fred).
    • b. ∀yF(Evelyn,y).
    • c. ∀x∃yF(x,y).
    • d. ¬∃x∀yF(x,y).
    • e. ∀y∃xF(x,y).
    • f. ¬∃x(F(x,Fred) ∧ F(x,Jerry)).
    • g. ∃x∃y(F(Nancy,x) ∧ F(Nancy,y) ∧ ∀z(F(Nancy, z) → (z = x ∨ z = y)).
    • h. ∃!y∀xF(x,y).
    • i. ¬∃xF(x,x).
    • j. ∃x∃!y(F(x,y) ∧ x ≠ y).

    Here is a real mind-wrencher! If F(x,y,z) = x can fool y at z, where the domain of x and y is all people, and the domain of z is all times, then symbolize: You can fool all of the people some of the time, and you can fool some of the people all of the time, but you cannot fool all of the people all of the time.
