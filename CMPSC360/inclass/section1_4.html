  • 18.

    D = {-2,-2,0,1,2}

    • a. ∃xP(x) ≡ P(-2) ∨ P(-1) ∨ P(0) ∨ P(1) ∨ P(2).
    • b. ∀xP(x) ≡ P(-2) ∧ P(-1) ∧ P(0) ∧ P(1) ∧ P(2).
    • c. ∃x¬P(x) ≡ ¬P(-2) ∨ ¬P(-1) ∨ ¬P(0) ∨ ¬P(1) ∨ ¬P(2).
    • d. ∀x¬P(x) ≡ ¬P(-2) ∧ ¬P(-1) ∧ ¬P(0) ∧ ¬P(1) ∧ P(2).
    • e. ¬∃xP(x): same as d (equivalent by DeMorgan's laws of negated quatifiers).
    • f. ¬∀xP(x): same as c (equivalent by DeMorgan's laws of negated quatifiers).

  • 32.
    • a. ∀x(D(x) → F(x)). Negation: ¬∀x(D(x) → F(x)) ≡ ∃x¬(D(x) → F(x)) ≡ ∃x¬(¬D(x) ∨ F(x)) ≡ ∃x(D(x) ∧ ¬F(x)). In English, the negation is: Some dogs do not have fleas.
    • b. ∃x(H(x) ∧ A(x)). Negation: ¬∃x(H(x) ∧ A(x)) or ∀x(H(x) → ¬A(x)). In English: No horses can add; alternatively: All horses cannot add.
    • c. Similar to a: ∀x(K(x) → C(x)). Negation: ¬∀x(K(x) → C(x)). In English: Some koalas cannot climb.
    • d. ¬∃x(M(x) ∧ F(x)). Negation: ∃x(M(x) ∧ F(x)). In English: Some monkeys can speak French.
    • e. Somewhat similar to b: ∃x(P(x) ∧ S(x) ∧ F(x)). Negation in English: No pigs can swim and catch fish.
